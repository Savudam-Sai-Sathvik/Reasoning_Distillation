Dataset generation:
I have tried various ways to get the good quality chain of thought from the teacher. This is essential because the entire pipeline depends on good quality data from the teacher model. 
The prompts I tried are(for 2000 samples per languages):
Simple step by step CoT. (2743/10000 correct predictions)
Socratic prompt, I asked to evaluate each option and question whether it applied the correct logic, actual words or assumptions. Summarise and think if it has eliminated options too quickly. (3452/10000 correct predictions)
For multilingual reasoning I asked to translate questions and options into English and then do socratic reasoning. ( 3647/10000 correct predictions)

These are two experiments I did, one is a linguistically balanced dataset and the other is an English heavy dataset with 60%. The reason I thought the goal of distillation is to learn the reasoning part from the teacher to solve the MCQ. English has high accuracy so it makes more correct answers, and it can be directly transferable to other languages. After training and testing the difference is not high, the balanced dataset slightly improved the accuracy.  The reason is that Qwen 2.5 1.5B has reasonable accuracy of 22% before distillation.

I tried offpolicy Distillation with forward KL divergence plus SFT
The reason I tried this is because simple SFT is good when the teacher's reasoning is really accurate. Correct answers do not imply good reasoning. Adding the KL term with temperature = 2 makes the student learn that the teacher doesn't know everything, it is uncertain, so it won’t blindly hallucinate. 


KL improves accuracy for low data resource languages like Tamil and Kannada.  But making an underconfident student on less data and on multilingual MCQ answering is not desired as you can see in the case of Llamma training for 2 epochs improving the performance.  We can’t use KL for cross family distillation as the tokens don’t match because of different tokenizers used during pretraining. We can only do the next token prediction over the reasoning of the teacher. So the only way to get good accuracy is to get a high quality reasoning dataset from the teacher. 

More experimentations would have concluded the findings, the other methods might be online distillation, I didn't have the time to implement it.
